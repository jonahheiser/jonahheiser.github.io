Outdoor Solar Charging Station

For the past 3 semesters, I have been leading a team of 6 students through the design and production of an outdoor solar charging station. This station is set to be delpoyed on Yale's campus in March of 2024.

Concept

The device will mimic the shape and functionality of a picnic table umbrella so that it can be inserted and removed into tables at will. Angled solar panels at the top of the device will collect solar energy and convert it into electricity, as well as provide a small amount of shade and rain cover. The project’s main goal is to support Yale’s movement toward deploying clean energy and to provide a highly-visible space that allows users to interact with simple solar-powered systems to increase user familiarity and comfortability when engaging with clean energy systems. A secondary goal of the project is to promote outdoor use for students by making it easier for students to stay connected while working outdoors.

Specs

The panels are 12V 100 W panels, and we have four of them to make up a system of 400W. Each panel is rated to be able to produce ~450 Watt-hours (Wh) per day (depending on sun exposure). The battery used is a 12V 100 Ah LiFEPO4 battery, with a maximum capacity of 1200 Wh. The sizing of the battery is such that there will be enough energy to support the charging of 5 laptops and 5 phones each day for three days, assuming the battery is not charged by the panels at all during this period.
